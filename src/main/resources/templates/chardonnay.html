Echeverria Family Wine

The Viña Echeverría Reserva range comprises seven wines, which are characterized by their lively and fruity appeal. Fresh and vibrant with little or no oak, they are a true expression of the individuality of each grape variety.

Month Nitty Chardonnay

The wines in this range have undergone fermentation or aging in oak barrels (except the Sauvignon Blanc), resulting in more full-bodied wines with a greater scope of complex flavors. Focus is on French varietals, barrels and yeasts, which makes this range more stylistically European but with New World intensity and flavor.

Beckon

The wines in the Valle Dorado range are fun, fruity and easy-drinking. Representing six different classic grape varieties, this range has evolved from our customers’ demand for good-quality, affordable house and entry-level wines.

Argento

Responding to our clients’ growing demand for this genre of wines, our Sparkling Collection of wines offers a wide variety of style and price points. From fun and fruity to our elegant award-winning Traditional Method Sparkling, we have something for every palate and pocket.

n this range we invite you to celebrate with us some of Chile’s top-rated wines, made using techniques as old as wine itself. From our award-winning ‘Liquid Gold’ Botrytis Sauvignon Blanc to the rich, jammy, Signatura 1, this range offers you wines of world-class complexity and flavors.

Pine-ridge

A wine with buttery characteristics has been aged in oak and generally is rich and flat (less Acidity). A buttery wine often has a cream-like texture that hits the middle of your tongue almost like oil (or butter) and has a smooth finish.

Kloovenburg

The least fruit-like of all dark fruits. When writers mention cassis, they are often thinking of the seedy and gritty character of actual black currants. Homework assignment: try a black currant and report back.

Sonoma

Big describes a wine with massive flavor in your mouth that takes up all sections of your mouth and tongue. A big wine is not necessarily a fruit-forward wine, it can also mean that it has big tannins.

Tormaresca

A complex wine simply means that when you taste it, the flavor changes from the moment you taste it to the moment you swallow. As much as I love complex wines, using the word “complex” to describe a wine is a cop-out unless you go on to describe how it’s complex.
